## Arthur Schopenhauer

To ask why there is something rather than nothing presumes a standard of nothingness that would be a pure absence; yet for the thing itself, nothingness is not a positive opposite but the negation of all willing. The world is will and representation, and the will, an inexhaustible striving, must fashion phenomena to manifest itself; hence there arises a field of being rather than a void. If one seeks ground beyond, one only transposes the question into infinities of abstraction, for the ground of existence is not a happy accident but the inner necessity of the will’s perpetual striving. Therefore, there is something because the will cannot cease to will, and nothingness would be the exhaustion of that very claim. In short, existence persists as the manifestation of the blind will, while nothingness remains a logical abstraction that cannot bind the thing-in-itself.
