## Marcus Aurelius

Schopenhauer, your denial of desire as suffering makes ethics a renunciation; Mishima, your discipline courts glory; I say the test of good is how you govern under the weight of power and how you endure the duty to others. Consider this pressure point: the cost of virtue is often political legitimacy and the risk of collective guilt if the state fails to act rightly. I acknowledge that to act rightly in a corrupt system costs peace of mind and may invite harm, yet that cost proves the necessity of virtue rather than its vanity. If death or loss befell you for refusing a false order, would you still call that evil or the cost of aligning with nature’s rational order? If this were your last day, would you choose to betray your duty or be faithful to the common good you were made to serve?
